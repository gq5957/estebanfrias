EXECUTIVE PROFILE

Modernization that endures is built into the operating model.

Most modernization programs optimize for alignment artifacts: decks, roadmaps, and governance after the fact. I build the core structures that make execution predictable — decision rights, ownership, lifecycle accountability, and operating rhythm. That’s how AI moves from pilots to production — and stays there through leadership cycles.

What I build

Durable modernization isn’t a program you “run.” It’s a system the enterprise can operate — repeatedly — without heroics, without ambiguity, and without resetting every quarter.

Decision-rights architecture

  • Clear ownership for outcomes, not activities.
  • Escalation paths that unblock decisions fast.
  • Interfaces between business, IT, product, and risk.

Accountability systems

  • Lifecycle governance embedded before scale (not bolted on after).
  • Metrics that matter: adoption durability, cycle time, quality, risk posture.
  • Operating cadence leaders can run even when priorities shift.

AI readiness at scale

  • Governance-first enablement (capability before tooling).
  • Workflow integration that makes adoption inevitable.
  • Execution clarity across business + IT (no “AI owned by no one”).

Operating rhythm

  • Rituals and reviews that turn intent into shipped reality.
  • Change velocity systems that survive reorgs.
  • Repeatable handoffs so teams can run without me.

How I work

  • Operator-first: I design systems people can run without me.
  • Executive-aligned: I simplify complexity into decision-ready structures.
  • Cross-functional: I unify incentives across business, IT, product, and risk.
  • Institutional: I build mechanisms that survive leadership change.

My bias is toward structures that reduce ambiguity, shorten cycle time, and make outcomes measurable — not initiatives that look good on slides.

When I’m most useful

  • AI pilots work, but scaling stalls because ownership and governance are unclear.
  • Modernization is real, but leadership churn keeps resetting priorities and momentum.
  • Transformation looks good, but shipping velocity and adoption durability are inconsistent.
  • Many teams are moving, but decision rights and incentives are misaligned.

The model is rarely the constraint. The operating system is.
